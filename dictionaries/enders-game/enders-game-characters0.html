Alai

Andrew "Ender" Wiggin

The protagonist, a brilliant child strategist drafted into Battle School who unknowingly commands humanity's final victory over the Formics.

Bean

The smallest and one of the smartest soldiers, whom Ender isolates to make him stronger; he becomes a key toon leader in Dragon Army and in the final war.

Bernard

A bully in Ender's launch group at Battle School whose control Ender breaks with computer pranks and fake messages.

Bonzo Madrid

The proud commander of Salamander Army who forbids Ender to fight and later leads six boys to attack Ender in the bathroom, where he is accidentally killed.

Carn Carby

Commander of Rabbit Army, the first to face Ender's Dragon Army; he respects Ender and tells him to beat the others soundly.

Colonel Hyrum Graff

Principal of Battle School who deliberately isolates and pressures Ender to shape him into a commander.

Crazy Tom

British veteran who leads C Toon in Dragon Army and is known for his temper with commanders he considers stupid.

Dap

The officer who meets Ender when he first arrives at Battle School and serves as his initial guide.

Demosthenes

Valentine Wiggin's pseudonym as an influential political essayist whose rabble-rousing writings contrast with Locke's.

Dink Meeker

A toon leader who doubts the war is real but stays for the game; he refuses command twice and eventually becomes one of Ender's trusted commanders.

Dumper (Champi T'it'u)

Peruvian soldier who leads E Toon in Dragon Army and is among the most loyal to Ender.

Fly Molo

Filipino soldier who leads A Toon in Dragon Army and initially doubts Ender's five-toon strategy before accepting it.

Han Tzu (Hot Soup)

Chinese soldier who leads D Toon in Dragon Army and later serves under Ender at Command School during the Third Invasion.

John Paul Wiggin

Ender's father, who agreed to have a third child for the International Fleet's Battle School program.

Locke

Peter Wiggin's pseudonym as a calm, statesmanlike political essayist whose writings pair with Demosthenes to shape global opinion.

Major Anderson

Graff's second-in-command at Battle School who runs the Battle Room and sometimes questions Graff's methods.

Mazer Rackham

The legendary commander who stopped the Second Formic Invasion and later trains Ender at Command School, revealed as the one running the "final exam."

Peter Wiggin

Ender's cruel, manipulative older brother who writes as Locke and aspires to rule Earth.

Petra Arkanian

The only girl in Salamander Army when Ender joins; a gifted shooter who later serves in Ender's Dragon Army and his jeesh at Command School.

Pinual

A former Battle School student who committed suicide after the Giant's Drink in the Mind Game; mentioned by the staff as a caution.

Pol Slattery

Commander of Leopard Army who surprises Salamander with new tactics and forces a draw when Ender disobeys Bonzo and fights alone.

Rose de Nose

Commander of Rat Army when Ender is transferred there; the only Jewish commander at the time and more fair to Ender than Bonzo.

Shen

A launchy in Ender's group who is mocked by Bernard until Ender befriends him and becomes his first friend at Battle School.

Stilson

A bully at Ender's school on Earth who gangs up on Ender; Ender defeats him so thoroughly that Stilson later dies from his injuries.

Talo Momoe

Commander of Tiger Army in the two-on-one battle against Dragon Army; he favors a direct frontal assault.

The Hive Queen

The Formic queen whose consciousness Ender encounters in the Mind Game and whose cocooned egg he discovers after the war.

Theresa Wiggin

Ender's mother, who struggles with letting Ender leave for Battle School.

Valentine Wiggin

Ender's compassionate older sister who stays in touch with him and later writes as the political pundit Demosthenes.

Vlad

Belarusian soldier who leads B Toon in Dragon Army and is one of the eleven chosen for Ender's jeesh in the war.

William Bee

Commander of Griffin Army in the two-on-one battle against Dragon Army alongside Tiger Army.
